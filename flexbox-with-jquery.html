• What is printmaking?
  • The different types
  • Some Printmakers

Printmaking is an artistic process of creating artworks by transferring an image from a prepared surface—a matrix (such as wood, metal, or screen)—onto another surface, usually paper or fabric, often allowing for multiple original impressions.

The different types of printmaking include relief printing, intaglio printing, planographic printing, and stencil-based printing.

Some notable printmakers include Robert Blackburn, Elizabeth Catlett, and Margaret Burroughs.
